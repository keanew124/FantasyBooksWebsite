What is fantasy?

Fantasy is a genre usually set in a fictional universe, often involving magic and different types of creatures. Some rather popular types of creatures include dragons, dwarves and elves. Some inspirations for this genre include mythology and fairy tales.

A Brief History

The roots of fantasy lie in old fairy tales and folklore, even though these stories are not deliberately called fantasy, they contain many fantastic elements. Works such as the Arabian Nights laid the foundation of the genre.

The genre was explicitly called fantasy starting in the 19th century, where authors such as George MacDonald started making fantasy novels.

In the 20th Century, authors such as JRR Tolkein and George RR Martin exploded the genre into popularity with works such as The Lord of the Rings and Game of Thrones
